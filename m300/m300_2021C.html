M300 C Mathematical Reasoning

Summer 2021

Course Summary

This is a first course in mathematical reasoning, largely focused on reading and writing rigorous mathematical proofs. As such, this is an introduction to the techniques commonly assumed in upper-level, proof-based math courses. Topics include logic, proof techniques, set theory, functions, and cardinality.

Textbook and Lecture Notes

There is no required textbook for this course. I will provide a set of lecture notes for each week, and post them in the course schedule below. However, we will be roughly covering the same content in the following free textbooks.

  • An Introduction to Mathematical Reasoning by Matthew Conroy and Jennifer Taggart
  • Mathematical Reasoning: Writing and Proof, Version 2.1 by Ted Sundstrom
